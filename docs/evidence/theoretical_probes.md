# Thesis-Relevant Probe Index

## Purpose

Short index of probes that may support the final thesis. Detailed commands,
metrics, and artifacts remain in the linked reports and run directories.

| Probe | What was tested | Scope and current result | Detailed record |
|---|---|---|---|
| End-to-end reward anchor | Complete perception → temporal representation → reward path versus a constant mean-reward baseline. | Two held-out file splits, seeds 42/43. The model beats baseline on both. This is reward-prediction evidence, not control evidence. | [Stage-2 reward pipeline](stage2_reward_pipeline.md); `runs/component_refinement/causal_transformer/02_vectorized_reward_anchor/` |
| Local reward-rate diagnostic | Whether predictions follow sustained driving progress despite sparse CarRacing reward pulses. | One human-driven qualitative trace; target uses a causal three-step reward average. Indicates progress tracking but optimistic no-progress predictions. Not a benchmark. | [Notebook 1](../../notebooks/01_reward_prediction_evidence.ipynb) |
| Nonlinear reward head | Linear reward readout versus `83 → 32 → 1` ReLU head. | Held-out reward prediction, seeds 42/43. Linear wins on both; nonlinear remains an optional unselected variant. This does not determine future Critic/Actor head capacity. | `runs/component_refinement/causal_transformer/03_nonlinear_reward_head/` |
| Adaptive Top-K selection | Learned K=8 patch candidates versus fixed-uniform and fixed-random K=8 candidate sets. | Held-out reward prediction, seeds 42/43. Learned selection wins against both static controls. Supports adaptive candidate selection, not semantic recognition, optimal K, or full inference savings. | `runs/component_refinement/causal_transformer/04_topk_selection_ablation/`; [run index](../../runs/component_refinement/causal_transformer/RUN_INDEX.md) |
| Top-K capacity | Learned Top-K at K=4, 8, 16, and 32 under the matched cached protocol. | Eight held-out runs, seeds 42/43. Mean baseline ratios: K=4 **0.886**, K=8 **0.806**, K=16 **0.822**, K=32 **0.870**. K=8 is retained as the reduced spatial-information default; K=16 is close but admits twice as many candidates. | `runs/component_refinement/causal_transformer/05_k_ablation/`; [implementation plan](../plans/implementation_plan.md) |
| Tokenizer eval policy | Deterministic posterior-mean vs stochastic posterior-sampling during evaluation (no retraining). | Two K=8 anchors, 10 no-retrain evals. Mean policy is bitwise reproducible and marginally better (lower MSE) than sample policy. Sample policy has low variance (ratio σ ≤ 0.004). Both pass 4/4 action probe. | `runs/component_refinement/causal_transformer/06_tokenizer_eval_policy/`; [results](../../runs/component_refinement/causal_transformer/06_tokenizer_eval_policy/RESULTS.md) |
| Temporal observational dropout | Visible-only K=8 reward anchors versus anchors trained with visible warmup and contiguous blind intervals. | Two seeds; every matched masked factual evaluation improved (30/30). Masked-trained ratios were 0.928–0.954 (seed 42) and 0.865–0.925 (seed 43), while visible reward quality remained below the constant-mean baseline. This supports short bounded blind reward prediction, not long-horizon imagination or control. | `runs/component_refinement/causal_transformer/08_masked_reward_anchor/`; [results](../../runs/component_refinement/causal_transformer/08_masked_reward_anchor/RESULTS.md) |
| Strict recurrent observational dropout | MinimalSRU anchors trained with 20 visible burn-in positions, 4 visible target positions, and target-relative blind intervals; masked images bypass perception. | Corrected two-seed evaluation: strict training improves every H=1/2/4/8/12 masked ratio over visible-only SRU and remains below the constant baseline. Mean strict masked ratios are 0.920/0.953 versus visible-only 0.954/1.023. Correct-vs-shifted action timing remains marginal, and strict execution is slower at the current low masked fraction. This supports bounded recurrent reward prediction, not imagined control. | [SRU strict results](sru_temporal/08_strict_observational_dropout_results.md) |
| SRU retained perception components | Learned K=8 versus fixed-random K=8, learned K=8 versus K=16, and posterior-mean versus sampled tokenizer inference under the same strict SRU protocol. | Two seeds and matched resources. Learned K=8 is better under blind intervals (masked ratio 0.937 versus 0.966) despite fixed-random winning visible regression; K=16 gives no masked gain (0.943); tokenizer mean is numerically deterministic and marginally better on average. Retain learned K=8 and mean inference for the S5 control probe. | [SRU retained-component results](sru_temporal/09_retained_components_results.md) |
| z-only SRU imagination and frozen control | Whether a recurrent `z_t` alone can advance blind dynamics and train Actor/Critic without changing the world model. | 2,000 updates in 49.1 s; Critic median loss fell 0.0495→0.0159 and all freeze/Polyak gates passed. Locked real return was −82.3 versus −92.9 zero action and −75.3 causal Actor. This establishes mechanical and predeclared parity, not useful driving: random actions scored −28.2 and the Actor remained nearly state-invariant. | [SRU S5 results](sru_temporal/10_frozen_actor_critic_parity.md) |
| Temporal-backend decision | Causal bounded history versus MinimalSRU recurrent state across factual reward, blind dynamics, temporal cost, and frozen control. | MinimalSRU selected: 9.6× fewer temporal parameters, about 9.2× faster incremental CPU step, comparable factual reward quality, genuine recurrent observational dropout, and frozen-policy parity within the declared tolerance. Causal code remains reproducible on its baseline branch. | [completed SRU plan](../plans/sru_temporal_validation_plan.md) |
| Critic-shaped shared representation | Whether factual reward anchors plus Critic pressure can update ControllerTrunk while Actor, SRU, and perception remain frozen. | One bounded seed-42 run: visible and masked held-out ratios improved slightly, while real return improved from −82.35 to −71.44 with bitwise-unchanged Actor weights. Supports the mechanism at the first boundary; cross-seed replication and deeper unfreezing remain pending. | [Stage 6.1 gate](joint_training/stage6_1_controller_critic.md) |
